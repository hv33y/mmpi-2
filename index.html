What is the MMPI-2?

The Minnesota Multiphasic Personality Inventory (MMPI) is the most widely used and researched, standardized clinical assessment tool. It is used to help diagnose adult personality and mental health disorders.
Various versions of the MMPI are used by psychologists and other mental health experts to help develop treatment plans, aid with differential diagnosis, assist with legal inquiries (forensic psychology) or screen job applicants during the selection process.
The MMPI was originally created in the 1930s. The test has been updated and modified multible times to improve accuracy and validity.
